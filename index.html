Raag

In Indian classical music, we are very familar with the word RAAG.
Many musicians have been performing the same raag with various lyrics so,
we came to know that raag is not about lyrics.
Many exprienced musicians say that learning all the raags is not possible.
Now, the question comes;

What is a raag and why can't we learn all of them?h2>

So basically, a raag in The Indian Classical Music is a combination of nodes
which forms a tune that depends on several major rules:

(1) A raag must have minimum 5 nodes. A rag containing 5 nodes in called Audav.
(2) When it has 6 nodes it is called as Shadow.
(3) A raag can have maximum 7 nodes then it is said to be Sampoorn.
(4) The nodes used in the rag may deffer from aaroh to avroh.

(These AUDAV, SHADOW and SAMPOORN are called as the cast of the particular raag.)

We cannot learn all the raags because following the above rules we get
a number of combinations of nodes which may form a thousands of raag
and learning all of them is not possibe for a normal human being.

Going in detail about rags ~

The raags have their own time on which they are suppose to be sung.
All raags have their own nature.
Raags can be performed on different beats ( taal ) like ~ TEENTAAL, TILWADATAAL etc.
There are some more terms called vadi, samvadi, anuvadi, thaat and vivadi.

Vadi: it is the most used node in the raag.
Samvadi: it is the second most used node in the raag.
Anuvadi: except vadi and samvadi rest nodes are called as anuvadi.
Thaat: it is the origin of the raag. Indian Classical Music have 10 of them.
Vivadi: vivadi nodes are not to be played during an improvisation on a raag.
